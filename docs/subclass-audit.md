# D&D 5e Subclass Audit & Gap Analysis

**Date:** May 13, 2026  
**Source:** [D&D Fanatics Complete Guide (Updated June 2024)](https://dungeonsanddragonsfan.com/all-dnd-5e-classes-subclasses-complete-guide/)

## Summary

According to the official count as of June 2024, there are **117 official DnD 5e subclasses** across 13 classes. Our current implementation has **61 subclasses** across 12 classes (missing Artificer entirely).

**Missing: 56 subclasses + 1 entire class (Artificer with 4 subclasses) = 60 total gaps**

## Additional Data Concerns

### Subclass Features & Abilities
The resource mentions that subclasses grant:
- **Spells:** Many subclasses grant additional spells or expanded spell lists
- **Abilities:** Unique features gained at specific levels (usually 3rd, 6th, 10th, 14th for most classes)
- **Stat Increases:** Some subclasses provide ASI (Ability Score Improvements) or stat bonuses
- **Proficiencies:** Additional weapon, armor, tool, or skill proficiencies

### Current Implementation Gap
Our current `scripts/subclasses-data.ts` only stores:
- `name`
- `description`
- `subclass_level`

### What We Need to Add
For each subclass, we should track:
1. **Features by Level:** What abilities/features are gained at each level
2. **Spell Lists:** Expanded spell lists or domain/oath/patron spells
3. **Stat Bonuses:** Any ASI or stat improvements
4. **Proficiency Grants:** Additional proficiencies gained
5. **Resource Pools:** Ki points, sorcery points, channel divinity uses, etc.
6. **Passive Benefits:** Always-on bonuses like AC improvements, movement speed, etc.

## Notes

- **Critical Role Content:** The resource mentions several Matt Mercer subclasses (Gunslinger, Cobalt Soul, Open Sea) that appear on D&D Beyond but are considered unofficial/third-party. We should probably skip these unless specifically requested.
- **2024 vs 2014 PHB:** Some subclasses have been renamed or updated (e.g., Totem Warrior → Wild Heart). We should use the latest names.
- **Villainous Options:** Death Domain (Cleric) and Oathbreaker (Paladin) are "Villainous Class Options" from the DMG. Consider marking these as "evil" or "DM approval required" in our UI.
